About me

I was born and raised in southern Ontario, and completed all my postsecondary studies at the University of Waterloo (BSc, 2020; MSc, 2022; PhD, 2026). I have always had a passion for astronomy, which has lead to my current work studying galaxy evolution through the lens of star formation. I am also an eager science communicator, and have spent time giving observatory tours at the Gustav Bakos Observatory, assisting with running Astro-Bubble planetarium events, hosted a series of public astronomy talks at the local library, and given several public talks of my own.

Outside of astronomy, I have a variety of hobbies and interested including fishing (especially ice fishing), rock climbing, and cooking. I've always fished freshwater, so I'm exctied for the chance to try out saltwater fishing when I move to Vancouver Island this fall!
